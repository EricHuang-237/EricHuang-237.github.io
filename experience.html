The effect of aminated graphene on the growth and development of neurons

Advisor: Meili Liu
Status: published
  • This study investigated the effects of aminated and carbonylated modified graphene on neurons and explored the effect of them at different concentrations on neuronal survival and growth. It was determined that 2μg/mL aminated graphene has the best promoting effect on neuron growth.
  • Using graphene in electrospinning technology to prepare the neural catheter stent materials, we explore the effect of using graphene and silk fibroin with different ratios on the survival and growth of neurons and determined the 3% ratio of graphene/silk fibroin Protein materials have good mechanical and electrical properties and contribute to the development of neurites.

Effect of nano-hydroxyapatite on the repair of spinal cord injury in rats

Advisor: Meili Liu
Status: published
  • We designed a platform for stretching the rat spine to make a mechanical injury (10% maximum amplitude and 60mm/h pulling speed)
  • We prepared a kind of coating material using nano-hydroxyapatite and successfully promoted the regeneration of the injured spine.

The inhibitory effect of SIRT1 molecule on flowing shear stress-induced neuronal apoptosis

Advisor: Meili Liu; Wei Song
Status: published
  • In this project, we first applied flow shear forces of different strengths to neurons, and found that a flow shear force of 10 dyn/cm2 had a significant effect of promoting apoptosis on neurons.
  • We transfected neurons with adenoviruses containing SIRT1-RNAi and wild-SIRT1 plasmids, and then applied flowing shear stress on the transfected neurons. It was found that SIRT1 can significantly inhibit the apoptotic effect induced by flowing shear stress.

Using Micro-pattern printing technology to control the growth of neurons

Advisor: Meili Liu
Status: published
  • In this subject, a dumbbell-shaped micropattern was designed according to the morphological characteristics of neurons.
  • According to the diameter of the neuron cell body, the micropatterns with side lengths of 30μm and 40μm were designed, and the corresponding PDMS stamps were prepared.
  • Using FN and Laminin as the “inkpad” to culture neurons, it was found that the 40μm dumbbell shape can significantly increase the connection and guidance between the protrusions, and promote the formation of a planar network of neurons.

The effect of combined force and electricity stimulation on neuron growth and development

Advisor: Meili Liu
Status: published
  • We designed and produced a combined force-electric stimulation device that can apply electric field stimulations of different types, strengths, and frequencies to multiple groups of cultured cells while maintaining static tension on the cells, thereby roughly simulating the mechanics and mechanics of spine development.
  • We chose 50-500mV external DC voltage and 2.5%-10% stretch amplitude as stimulation conditions, and observed the condition of neurons in each group. The expression of the neural guidance molecule Netrin1 and its inhibitory receptor UNC5 was tested. Conditions suitable for neuron growth and development were proposed.

A bladder cancer test strip using APOA1 as a tumor marker

Advisor: Lisha Zheng
Status: published
  • We investigated the current status of clinical diagnosis of bladder cancer and found that the current lack of a non-invasive diagnosis method, so we designed a colloidal gold test strip that can detect bladder cancer through urine samples.
  • We selected APOA1 as a tumor marker, and the positive/negative control test proved that the test strip can detect bladder cancer.
